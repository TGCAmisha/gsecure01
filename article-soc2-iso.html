From SOC 2 to ISO 27001: mapping controls without redoing the work

If you already have a clean SOC 2 Type II, you are 70% of the way to ISO 27001. The trick is mapping the gap precisely instead of starting fresh.

James O'ConnorVP, GRC Practice

Most companies that come to us for ISO 27001 already have SOC 2. They are nervous about a second standard because they assume it means double the work. It does not. Here is how we typically scope the move.

What carries over for free

The control concepts behind both standards overlap significantly. If you have credible evidence for these SOC 2 areas, you can reuse it almost wholesale:

  • Access management, joiner-mover-leaver, MFA, privileged access
  • Change management and SDLC controls
  • Vendor risk management
  • Incident response process and tabletop history
  • Logging, monitoring, and SIEM operations
  • Backup and restoration testing
  • Encryption at rest and in transit

What ISO 27001 wants that SOC 2 didn't push hard on

An information security management system, formally

SOC 2 lets you demonstrate operational controls. ISO 27001 wants the management system around them: policy, scope, leadership commitment, objectives, internal audit, management review. If your SOC 2 program runs on quarterly compliance check-ins, you have an ISMS in spirit. You just need to formalize it on paper.

A real risk assessment methodology

SOC 2 references risk; ISO 27001 wants a documented method, applied consistently, with a risk register tied to your statement of applicability. Allow two weeks for a workshop with the leadership team to define your risk criteria, plus a week to back-fill the register from existing knowledge.

The risk register is the document auditors flip to first. Make it real, make it short, and update it.

Statement of Applicability

The SoA maps Annex A controls to your environment, with justification for each. There is no SOC 2 equivalent. Plan a week to draft, a week to refine.

What to do, in order

  1. Map your existing SOC 2 controls to Annex A. A spreadsheet with three columns: SOC 2 reference, Annex A reference, evidence pointer. This is the project plan in disguise.
  2. Identify the genuine gaps. Typically 15-25 Annex A controls without a SOC 2 analog. Most are policy or governance, not technical.
  3. Stand up the ISMS framework. Policy, scope, objectives, internal audit calendar, management review cadence.
  4. Run an internal audit. Use an external partner if you do not have an independent function in-house.
  5. Stage 1 audit, then stage 2. Most certified bodies will compress this for SOC 2-mature organizations.

Realistic timeline

For a SOC 2 Type II-mature company with 100-300 employees, we typically deliver Stage 2 in five months from kickoff. Companies that try to do this without leveraging the SOC 2 work tend to take eight to twelve.

The frameworks are different in shape but not in substance. If you have invested in SOC 2 properly, ISO 27001 is closer than it looks.
